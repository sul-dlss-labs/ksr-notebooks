Chicago police today were investigating the apparent suicide of a 28-year-old man who they said shot himself after accidentally shooting his 18-year-old brother in the head.

The brothers were in the 1300 block of West 71st Street about midnight when the older brother tried to shoot a dog, but the bullet struck the younger man instead, police said. Police said they believe the dog had bitten the younger man.

The older brother -- identified as Brandon Thomas of the 14400 block of South Dearborn Street, Riverdale -- took off in a car and was stopped by police in the 7000 block of South Halsted Street in Chicago.

As officers approached the vehicle, Thomas put a gun to his head and fired, police said. He was pronounced dead a short time later in Advocate Christ Medical Center in Oak Lawn, according to the Cook County medical examiner's office.

As of Monday, his brother was still alive, in critical condition in the same hospital, police and the medical examiner's office said.

Wentworth Area detectives are investigating.
