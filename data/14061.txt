TUSTIN – Authorities have identified a man who was shot and killed by officers responding to a domestic disturbance at an apartment complex.

Robert Miguel Gutierrez Villa, 23, was shot during a confrontation with officers in a residential area near Red Hill Avenue and Nisson Road shortly after 11:30 a.m. on Monday, Tustin police Lt. John Strain said.

Multiple officers fired their weapons at Villa, who police say was armed with a knife.

Villa was taken to Western Medical Center in Santa Ana, where he was pronounced dead several hours later.

According to Orange County Superior Court records, a man with the same name and birth date as Villa had several previous run-ins with the law, including pleading guilty to felony burglary, felony inflicting corporal injury and misdemeanor violating a protective order in 2012, and pleading guilty to misdemeanor inflicting corporal injury on a spouse or significant other in 2011.

Villa’s death is being investigated by the Orange County District Attorney’s Office, as is routine for shootings involving officers.
