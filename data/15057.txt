St. Augustine Record

A St. Johns County Sheriff's deputy shot and killed a man at a home on Race Track Road on Saturday morning.

The deputy was not injured and the victim was Aaron Ray, 43, according to Sheriff's Office spokesman Kevin Kelshaw.

Detectives with the Sheriff's Office Major Crimes Unit are investigating an officer involved shooting incident that occurred outside a residence in the 2600 block of Race Track Road., off S.R. 16.

Deputies responded to a report of a domestic disturbance involving an aggravated battery with shots fired at the residence at 1:30 a.m. Responding deputies were advised by the female victim that the male suspect had possibly barricaded himself inside the residence.

Deputies with the Crisis Negotiation Team along with SWAT Team members responded to the residence. While establishing a perimeter, deputies encountered the armed suspect, who refused to drop the weapon and shots were fired.

Deputies attempted to render aid before Fire/Rescue personnel arrived, however, the suspect, identified as Aaron Ray, 43, was pronounced deceased at the scene.

The two deputies involved were Cpl. Shawn Emert and Deputy Jason Kroul, both members of the Sheriff's Office SWAT team. They were placed on administrative leave, which is standard procedure when an officer is involved in a shooting.

They were not injured.
