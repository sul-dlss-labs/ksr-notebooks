SOUTHFIELD, MI -- State police on Monday identified a man killed in a Sunday motorcycle crash as Bassam Issa, 27, of Sterling Heights.

Police were trying to stop a Kawasaki motorcycle on I-696 near Lahser Road around 7:30 p.m. when the vehicle struck a 2005 Chevrolet Silverado.

The driver of the motorcycle, according to police, had refused to stop and was "traveling shoulder to shoulder" before running into the truck in the right lane.

He was taken by the Southfield Fire Department to a local hospital, where he was pronounced dead, according to police.

No one else was was injured in the crash.

Issa was wearing helmet, police said.

An autopsy and toxicology reports are pending.
