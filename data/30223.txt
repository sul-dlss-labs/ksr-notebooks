EAST CLEVELAND, Ohio (WOIO) - One man is dead after an East Cleveland police pursuit led to a fatal and fiery crash.

27-year-old Shawn Antoine Crenshaw died as a result of injuries sustained in the fire, East Cleveland police said.

The pursuit began around 12:35 a.m. on Saturday near a Marathon Gas Station on Noble and Euclid in East Cleveland.

East Cleveland police said they pursued the car as apart of a drug investigation.

Police chased the car to E. 110th Street and St. Clair Avenue in Cleveland, according to a Detective Commander on the case. The car chase ended when the suspect crashed into a utility pole.

Police said the suspect’s vehicle burst into flames following the crash. Body camera footage shows East Cleveland officers pulling the driver from the car.

Video shows the suspect, identified by police as 31-year-old Ryan Pete Lavell Clemmons Sr., bleeding from the head.

Below is a section of a statement from East Cleveland Police Chief Scott Gardner:

“Officers could be heard on body-camera multiple times asking the driver if anyone else was in the car. Officers attempted to clear the vehicle to see if anyone else was inside, yet they were unable to do so because of the fire.”

Crenshaw Jr. was a passenger inside the vehicle.

Officers found two loaded firearms in Clemmons’ possession, and he was arrested and taken to the hospital.

He’s currently charged with aggravated vehicular homicide, but East Cleveland police said other charges are likely.

Cleveland police are investigating the accident while East Cleveland police are investigating the incident that led to the police chase.

No East Cleveland police officers were injured amid this incident, police said.

Clemmons Sr. is expected to make a court appearance Wednesday, February 3, and could face additional charges.
