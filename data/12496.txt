By From Staff Reports

The man shot and killed by Ventura police Thursday night after injuring two officers was a 29-year-old Vancouver, Wash., resident, officials said.

Clifford Park struck two female officers with his pickup while trying to drive away from a Pierpont-area Motel 6 where he had caused a disturbance, authorities said. The officers later shot him.

Park died of gunshot wounds, according to the Ventura County Medical Examiner's Office.

(Read: Ventura police not sure exactly what sparked Motel 6 incident that left 1 dead, 2 officers injured)

The disturbance call was reported about 7:05 p.m. Thursday at the Motel 6 at 2145 E. Harbor Blvd.

One officer was hit by the pickup, treated at a hospital and released. The other officer was run over and admitted to a trauma center, where she was treated Friday for moderate to serious injuries, officials said.

Police said Friday they weren't sure why Park turned violent. More information on the incident was expected to be released Monday.
