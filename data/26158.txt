Wayne State University says that its police department engaged in a deadly shootout after receiving reports of a “man firing a gun from a bicycle” Wednesday night, June 5.

The school says that the suspect is dead and that there were no other injuries in the incident that took place about 11:24 p.m. at Third and Peterboro street in Detroit.

“The officer responded to reports of a man firing a gun from a bicycle," Wayne State writes in the release.

"Upon arrival, the man fired several shots at the officer, who returned fire.”

The officer involved in the shooting will be off duty while a full investigation into the incident is launched. The Detroit News reports that the officer involved in the shooting is a five-year veteran.

The man killed in the shootout has not yet been identified, but the News says he is believed to be in his 30s.
