A Dallas man was arrested Wednesday after police say he led officers on a chase in a carjacked vehicle, then crashed into two other vehicles â€” killing one of the occupants..

Benjamin Chavez, 26, was charged with murder, aggravated robbery, and evading arrest causing death.

Undercover officers spotted the vehicle shortly before 2 p.m. and alerted patrol officers to its location. When the patrol officers turned on their lights and sirens, Chavez sped off, police said.

Police cruisers and a helicopter followed him to the intersection of Bruton Road and Pleasant Drive, near Buckner Boulevard, where the he crashed into two other vehicles then ran away, according to police.

Two occupants of the other vehicles involved in the crash were hospitalized. Wayne Towns, 65, died of his injuries later that evening, authorities said. Towns was a Kaufman resident, InForney.com reported.

A witness directed officers to the backyard of a nearby home, where they took Chavez into custody. Chavez admitted to fleeing from officers and causing the crash, as well as to committing a robbery Tuesday, police said.

Chavez has previously pleaded guilty to burglary of a vehicle, evading arrest or detention, unlawful carrying of a weapon, aggravated assault with a deadly weapon, burglary of a habitation and possession of marijuana.

He remained in the Dallas County jail Thursday. Bail was set at $225,000.
