A man fleeing police on foot was struck and killed by a van on Interstate 35W in Minneapolis early Saturday morning.

Minneapolis Police say three suspects ran from a stolen car, after it crashed near Lake Street and 2nd Avenue during a police chase. The three ran onto the interstate, where two of them were caught by police in the northbound lanes.

But the third, identified by the Minnesota State Patrol as Jonathan Barbour, 25, jumped the median wall and was hit by a van driving in the center lane of southbound I-35W. Police say a handgun was found on the road near where he was struck.

Barbour was taken to Hennepin County Medical Center where he later died.

Police say Barbour had a felony warrant from the Department of Corrections stemming from a first-degree aggravated robbery charge.

The driver of the van was not injured.
