PORTLAND, Ore. (KOIN) — A 77-year-old man standing on his porch shooting at his neighbor’s house was shot to death by The Dalles police on March 17.

When officers and Oregon State Police troopers arrived at the scene on East 10th Street, James Young was still standing on his porch firing his gun. When they ordered him to put his rifle down, he fired multiple times at the officers.

One of the officers fired twice from about 75 feet away, striking and killing Young.

Investigators later determined Young shot multiple times into the kitchen area of the house next door. Two rifles and moree than two dozen shell casings were found on Young’s porch.

The Wasco County District Attorney prepared the summary of the investigation and will not convene a grand jury to investigate further. The officer was ruled justified in his use of deadly force.
