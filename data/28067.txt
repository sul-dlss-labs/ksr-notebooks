A suspect accused of shooting an East Baton Rouge Parish sheriff's deputy during a chase Sunday morning was shot and killed after an hours-long manhunt, according to Sheriff Sid Gautreaux and WVLA.

The suspect, 39-year-old Bernard Ledlow, was found in a wooded area near Kendalwood Road and Hoo Shoo Too Road, according to WVLA.

Homeowners in the perimeter were ordered to evacuate but WVLA said an exact number of affected households was not known.

The incident began when a sheriff's deputy attempted to pull Ledlow over 7 a.m. Sunday near Airline Highway and Pecue Road.

Authorities said a pursuit then began, with Ledlow reportedly firing shots out the back window of his vehicle before crashing at a dead end on Kendalwood Road.

Ledlow fled on foot, according to authorities, and continued to fire shots as he fled into a wooded area with a rifle.

A deputy received a graze wound to the leg and was taken to the hospital where he has since been discharged, according to a spokesperson.
