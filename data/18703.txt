Azusa police officer fatally shoots man who pointed handgun at him, authorities say

AZUSA, Calif. (KABC) -- An Azusa Police Department officer shot and killed a 49-year-old man Sunday evening after the suspect pointed a handgun at him, authorities said.

Police responded to a report of a man with a gun and shots fired in the 1800 block of Ranch Road at about 11:21 p.m., the department said in a statement. Officers arrived at the scene and contacted the man, who refused to obey their commands, investigators said.

The suspect then aimed a firearm at one of the officers, according to the news release.

"Fearing for his life, the officer fired several rounds from his duty weapon at the suspect," the statement said, "striking the suspect at least once in the upper torso."

The man was pronounced dead at the scene. He was not immediately identified.

In accordance with protocol, Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department detectives are assisting in an investigation.

Anyone with information about the incident is asked to call sheriff's Homicide Bureau at (323) 890-5500.
