GARDEN GROVE – Authorities on Friday identified the 16-year-old shot and killed by police Wednesday as Joao Palma.

The Orange County coroner’s office said the boy was shot in the head.

Police officials said the shooting happened after a robbery that officers witnessed about 9:30 p.m. Wednesday.

Undercover officers conducting surveillance near Galway Street and Garden Grove Boulevard saw a woman being robbed in an alley near the intersection

The officers spotted a white sedan leaving the area and the officers followed the sedan. The sedan stopped at a traffic light and the officers got out of their vehicle, identified themselves as officers and confronted the teens, police said.

One of the teens in the car – now identified as Palma – grabbed one of the officers and stabbed him with a knife, police said. The officer, who suffered minor injuries, shot and killed Palma.

The Santa Ana boy was pronounced dead at the scene.

The driver of the car then drove away, traveling several blocks to Magnolia Street and Garden Grove Boulevard, where police took a 16-year-old boy and a 16-year-old girl into custody.
