Two men were killed over the weekend in unrelated incidents after allegedly exchanging gunfire with area police officers.

On Saturday night, Chicago police shot and killed Rigoberto Lopez, 24, after he allegedly held three people hostage during a robbery attempt in an auto shop on the Southeast Side.

Chicago Police Sgt. Antoinette Ursitti said police got a call at about 7:50 p.m. about a possible robbery at an auto store in the 9600 block of South Ewing Avenue. When they arrived, Lopez refused to come out of the store and took three people inside hostage, authorities said.

Hostage and SWAT officers were brought to the scene, police said, and at about 9:30 p.m, Lopez allegedly fired several shots at officers and officers fired back.

Lopez of the 10300 block of South Muskegon Avenue was pronounced dead at the scene. Ursitti said the three hostages, whose identities were not released, were not harmed.

The Independent Police Review Authority will review the shooting, she said.

Also on Saturday, police in Markham shot and killed one man and wounded another.

At about 10:30 p.m., officers responded to a 911 call about two men with guns inside a Dollar Tree store in Canterbury Shopping Center, police said. When the officers arrived, two men were running out of the store, and one opened fire, police said.

The officers fired back, wounding one man and killing Antoine Covington, 18, of Markham.

The condition of the second man was unavailable. State police will review the shooting.
