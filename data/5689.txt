DES MOINES (AP) - A Nebraska man was killed and two other people were injured in a brief high-speed chase with a Polk County sheriff's deputy that ended when the driver of the car lost control and crashed, officials said.

Wilbert McDowell, 37, of Omaha, Neb., a passenger in the car, died at the scene, police said.

Another man, the driver whose identity wasn't immediately known, and a woman, Misty Belt, 27, who also was a passenger, were taken to the hospital with non-life threatening injuries, Des Moines police Sgt. Todd Dykstra case.

Belt's hometown wasn't immediately known.

The chase began about 9:30 p.m. Tuesday when the deputy tried to stop the car, having discovered it was stolen.

The driver fled, then lost control of the car. The vehicle sheared off a light pole and became wedged between some trees.
