NEW ORLEANS, La. (WVUE) - The New Orleans Police Department is investigating a traffic fatality involving a police officer Thursday night.

Officers responded to the intersection of General DeGaulle Drive and Westbend Parkway around 9:45 p.m. for a traffic incident.

According to NOPD’s investigation, an off duty NOPD officer, identified as Derrick Williams, was driving his department issued vehicle eastbound on General DeGaulle Dr. when a bicyclist riding northbound on Westbend Parkway rode into the officer’s lane. The bicyclist was struck by the vehicle.

The bicyclist, that has been identified as a 54-year-old man, was transported to a local hospital where he was later pronounced dead.

The name of the victim has not been released. The Orleans Parish Coroner’s Office will release the victim’s name and an official cause of death upon completion of an autopsy and notification of family.

NOPD says the officer was not injured in the crash.

As part of the NOPD’s policy, Williams was administered a drug and alcohol test that showed no signs of impairment.

Williams, who is a 24 year veteran, has been reassigned pending the outcome of the investigation.

If anyone has any information about the incident, they are asked to contact Traffic Fatality Investigator Sgt. Bernard Crowden at (504) 658-6208.
