Police now say a driver pointing a gun at an officer is what led to the officer-involved shooting in North Manchester on Tuesday.

Police initially pulled over 40-year-old Michael Kline for not signaling a turn.

At some point during the traffic stop, Kline reportedly got out of the truck and pointed a 9 mm handgun at the officer. That's when the officer fired at Kline.

While searching the truck, investigators found meth, marijuana and drug paraphernalia inside.
