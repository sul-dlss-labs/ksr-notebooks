A 66-year-old man died after he was struck by police officers responding to a call of gunshots fired in the Roseland neighborhood on the Far South Side, Chicago police said.

About 10:40 p.m. Monday, tactical officers from the Calumet District were heading north in the 10500 block of South Michigan Avenue when they hit a 1998 Honda coupe traveling west in the intersection of Michigan and 105th Street, police said.

The driver of the coupe, the 66-year-old man, was taken in critical condition to Advocate Christ Medical Center in Oak Lawn. He was later pronounced dead.

The Cook County medical examiner's office identified the man as Jack Burris, of the 8500 block of South Wabash Avenue. He was pronounced dead at Advocate Christ Medical Center about 11:25 p.m.

The officers had their emergency equipment at the time, according to a police statement. The officers in the crash were taken to Little Company of Mary Hospital for minor injuries.

The crash remains under investigation.
