WORCESTER - The Virginia murder suspect who shot himself in the head in Fitchburg on Wednesday has died, according to the Worcester County District Attorney's office.

Dana William, 43, was wanted on murder and kidnapping charges after he allegedly killed a 67-year-old man in Virginia and abducted the man's 68-year-old wife.

Authorities headed to a Fitchburg home Wednesday to apprehend William and he shot himself.

The Amelia County Sheriff's Office in Virginia posted on Facebook Wednesday that William succumbed to his injuries, but Worcester County District Attorney's office said he was still in critical condition. On Friday afternoon, the district attorney's office announced William had died.

William is accused of killing Woodell E. Brooks in Amelia County, Virginia last week. William allegedly abducted Brooks wife, Olene. She remains missing.
