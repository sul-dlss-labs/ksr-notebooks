NEWARK — A robbery suspect who was shot and killed by a Newark police officer Sunday afternoon has been identified as a 32-year-old Freehold man, authorities said.

Carlos Ribot was allegedly attempting to rob the Twin City Supermarket on Broadway in Newark's North Ward when he was intercepted by a police officer who was arriving for work there as a security guard, Acting Essex County Prosecutor Carolyn Murray said.

Ribot allegedly drew a handgun when the officer ordered him to stop, according to Anthony Ambrose, the prosecutor's chief of detectives. The officer shot Ribot once, and the 32-year-old died a short time later at University Hospital in Newark, Ambrose said.

The prosecutor's office is still reviewing the shooting, according to Ambrose, who said the officer was not harmed during the incident.
