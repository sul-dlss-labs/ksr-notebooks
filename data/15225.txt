BIRMINGHAM, Alabama -- A man shot and killed Friday by police in Warrior was wanted in a September killing in Indiana, according to The Indianapolis Star.

Adam Ardett Madison, 28, was shot and killed Friday afternoon near Warrior-Trafford Road. Warrior police said police were conducting a driver's license checkpoint and the officer got into a confrontation with Madison. The officer was forced to use deadly force, police said.

Madison was wanted by police in Indianapolis in the Sept. 18 shooting of 43-year-old Darrick Johnson, according to The Indianapolis Star.

Johnson died three weeks after the shooting, according to the Star.

The shooting in Warrior is being investigated by the Alabama Bureau of Investigation.
