Police have charged a Peitas man with manslaughter following a fatal car crash.

When Peitas police responded to the local Walmart on Dec. 30 to investigate a theft, the driver of a Toyota Camry fled north on Tom Gill Road.

Police chased the Camry, which eventually crashed near the intersection of Mile 2 North and Tom Gill.

The passenger, Cassandra Garcia, died as a result of the crash, according to the Peitas Police Department.

Her mother and sister identified Garcia by her tattoos, including ink below her left ear.

Police charged the driver, 45-year-old Arnoldo S. Ochoa of Peitas, with manslaughter, evading arrest or detention with a vehicle and reckless driving.

Ochoa TMs total bond was set at $450,000.

Manslaughter is a second-degree felony punishable by a maximum 20 years in prison and a fine not to exceed $10,000.

Evading arrest or detention with a motor vehicle is a third-degree felony punishable by a maximum 10 years in prison and a fine not to exceed $10,000. Reckless driving is a misdemeanor.
